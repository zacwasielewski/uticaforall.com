Issues

Kept Transparency a Top Priority

Right from the start, Celeste made the Council more transparent and more accountable. She insisted that they keep and post minutes, for example. She continuously defends public comment, even during the Pandemic when meetings were online. She also successfully argued for a strict version of the "Thursday Rule", so that Council members have time to study legislation before voting on it. She always insists that open meeting laws are followed, and works to keep all public business in front of the public, where it belongs. Now that the pandemic is over, she will continue to encourage people to come to Council meetings and use public comment.

Worked Hard to Address Flooding Problems

Since before even being sworn in, Celeste has worked to make sure that flooding remains a top priority in City Hall. She educated herself on how surface water works and why flooding happens. She regularly attends the Sauquoit Creek Basin Intermunicipal Commission. She has held three flooding meetings, and will be scheduling more for this Fall. She has developed good working relationships with both the DPW and Engineering Department in order to be able to both understand why flooding happens and how to prevent it. She is planning a meeting in September to update local residents on multiple efforts to mitigate for flooding.

Communication

Celeste communicates with constituents all the time, and in multiple ways. She gives out her personal phone number and picks up the phone whenever she can. She responds to emails, text messages, and Facebook messages. She posts regularly on Facebook and Twitter. AND she has sent out dozens and dozens of email Newsletters in the last year and a half, keeping anyone who signs up for them informed of Council and City business. She has also produced at least a dozen short videos (found on Facebook and YouTube) that promote South Utica and explain How Utica Works. Celeste fundamentally believes that transparency requires that elected officials communicate ALL THE TIME with the people they represent. She has several ideas for more "How Utica Works" newsletters, so stay tuned.

Helped to Maintain Livable Neighborhoods

Celeste goes to every ZBA meeting relevant to South Utica and advocates for neighbors. She has helped Constituents dealing with a wide variety of quality of life issues, from the need for "no parking" legislation and new stop signs, to writing legislation to deal with garbage and scrap metal. Celeste knows that quality of life in neighborhoods often comes down to enforcing our Codes, and she works hard with City Hall to ensure that this happens. She organized volunteers to help clean up neighborhoods for the Great American Cleanup. She even started a walking group that meets every Sunday and walks up into Roscoe Conkling Park. She is working hard to make sure that the gem of Roscoe Conkling Park is improved to provide more accessibility and enjoyment for everyone in Utica.

Economic Development in South Utica

Celeste has done a lot to promote South Utica businesses. She makes videos to promote local, independent businesses, as well as the arts. Celeste knows that there is a strong connection between economic development and supporting the arts. She will continue to work with the Uptown Theater to get them the funding they need, and will work with local business owners to begin an Uptown Business Association.
